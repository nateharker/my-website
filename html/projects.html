INTEX Fall 2020


This was the culminating project for the first semester of the BYU IS program. The challenge was to design a recruiting website that helps black job seekers overcome roadblocks that put them at a disadvantage in the recruiting process. Our solution incorporated elements of Python programming, SQL, Django web framework, a live PostgreSQL database, and a recommendation model. We then developed a prototype website and presented our propoal to the "potential employer."

EY Case


To start off the first semester of the BYU IS program, we worked on a week long business case with Ernst and Young. The case was to work with an imaginary company, VoydSec, to assess and strengthen their internal security posture. The deliverable was a report identifying key issues and risks in a previously performed penetration test, identifying key issues and risks in the change management process, analyzing login attempts during the time frame of a suspected security breach, and providing recommendations on how to mitigate all the risks identified. Global Superstore Analysis Using Tableau


The above visualizations were created to analyze the Global Superstore data in regards to their profitability in the United States. In the top left, it shows that California's profit has been following an upward trend for the last two years. In the top right, it shows that while they are losing money in several states, California and New York are the two most profitable states for Global Superstore. In the bottom left, it shows that Office Supplies is clearly the most profitable product category in California while furniture seems to not be very lucrative. In the bottom right, it shows that based on previous growth trends, Global Superstore can expect steadily increasing revenue in California over the next year of business.
